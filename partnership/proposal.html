The Problem

Duolingo retains users.
It does not make them fluent.

01

Apps teach recognition

They train you to pick correct answers. Language requires production — actually speaking, forming sentences, making and correcting mistakes in real time.

02

Adults need real conversation

Not a gamified exercise loop. Adults who need Spanish for work, travel, or life need something that adapts, responds, and pushes back — like a tutor does.

03

Engagement is not fluency

A user can complete 300 lessons and still not order coffee in Spanish. The product is sticky. The outcome is not. That gap is where Professor Madrid lives.

The Science

Built on research.
Not on vibes.

Every core feature maps directly to a proven body of learning science. This is not "AI-powered" as a marketing badge — it is applied cognitive research, translated into product.

Ebbinghaus · 1885

Spaced Repetition

Memory decays on a predictable curve. Strategic repetition flattens it permanently. Seagull Steven loops phrases at neurological depth — you don't memorize, you absorb.

Krashen · 1982

Comprehensible Input

Language is acquired, not learned — through exposure to input slightly above your current level. The Chat Tutor calibrates every response to push you forward without overwhelming you.

Varela et al. · 1991

Embodied Cognition

Words attached to real objects are retained 5× longer than flashcard vocabulary. Street View teaches Spanish from your actual surroundings — your brain stores it the way it stored your native language.
